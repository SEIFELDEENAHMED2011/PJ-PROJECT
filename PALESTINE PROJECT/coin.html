PALESTINE





Prior to the British Mandate and the establishment of a Palestinian currency, the situation with money in Palestine was quite mixed.
There wasn't a single currency specific to the region. Here's what you'd find:
Ottoman Currency: As Palestine was part of the Ottoman Empire for centuries, the dominant currency was the 'Ottoman lira'. This
would have been the main form of exchange for most transactions.
Multi-Currency Mix: Beyond the Ottoman lira, other currencies circulated in Palestine. This could include
currencies from neighboring Egypt (Egyptian pound) or even European powers like Britain, France, and Russia. Trade with
these countries would have brought their currencies into use.
So, before the occupation, Palestine lacked a single, unifying currency. It functioned with a mix of currencies depending
on trade and the historical context.
